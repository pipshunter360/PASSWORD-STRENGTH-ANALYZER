Security Tips
Expert guidance to protect your digital identity
Tip 01 / 10
Use a Password Manager
Never reuse passwords. A password manager generates and stores unique complex passwords for every site — you only remember one master password.
Enable 2FA Everywhere
Two-factor authentication adds a second layer. Even if your password leaks, attackers cannot access your account without the second factor.
Rotate Passwords Regularly
Update important passwords every 3–6 months. Immediately change any password for accounts that appeared in a known data breach.
Avoid Personal Info
Never use your name, birthdate, or phone number in passwords. This information is publicly available and trivial to guess or research.
Never Share Passwords
No legitimate service will ask for your password via email, phone, or chat. Treat any such request as a phishing attempt immediately.
Beware Public Wi-Fi
Avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public networks. Use a VPN if you must. Attackers on the same network can intercept unencrypted traffic.
