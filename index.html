Security Tips
Best practices for staying secure online
🔒
Use a Password Manager
Tools like Bitwarden, 1Password, or KeePass generate and store unique passwords for every site, so you only need to remember one master password.
📏
Length Beats Complexity
A 20-character password made of random words is far stronger than an 8-character complex one. Every extra character multiplies the difficulty exponentially.
🚫
Never Reuse Passwords
If one site is breached and you reuse passwords, attackers use "credential stuffing" to access all your other accounts automatically.
📱
Enable 2-Factor Authentication
Even if someone steals your password, 2FA (via an authenticator app, not SMS) makes your account nearly impossible to compromise.
🕵️
Watch for Phishing
Many breaches start with phishing emails. Always verify the sender's address and never enter your password on a suspicious page.
🔄
Update Compromised Passwords
Regularly check HaveIBeenPwned for your email. If a breach is detected, change that password immediately — and any account where it was reused.
🧱
Avoid Personal Information
Names, birthdays, pet names, and phone numbers are the first things attackers guess. They're easily found from social media and public records.
⌨️
Avoid Keyboard Patterns
Patterns like "qwerty", "123456", "asdfgh" are in every hacker's dictionary. Even substituting numbers (p@ssw0rd) doesn't help — those tricks are well known.
☁️
Secure Your Recovery Options
Your password reset email and phone number are as important as your password. Use a strong, unique password for your main email account above all else.
