Security Tips
Best practices every user should know
🔒
Use a Password Manager
Tools like Bitwarden, 1Password, or KeePass generate and store unique passwords for every site, so you only remember one master password.
📏
Length Beats Complexity
A 20-character password made of random words is far stronger than an 8-character complex one. Every extra character multiplies difficulty exponentially.
🚫
Never Reuse Passwords
If one site is breached and you reuse passwords, attackers use "credential stuffing" to access all your other accounts automatically.
📱
Enable 2-Factor Authentication
Even if someone steals your password, 2FA via an authenticator app makes your account nearly impossible to compromise.
🕵️
Watch for Phishing
Many breaches start with phishing emails. Always verify the sender's address and never enter your password on a suspicious page.
🔄
Update Compromised Passwords
Regularly check HaveIBeenPwned for your email. If a breach is detected, change that password immediately on all sites where it was used.
🧱
Avoid Personal Information
Names, birthdays, pet names, and phone numbers are the first things attackers guess. They're easily found from social media and public records.
⌨️
Avoid Keyboard Patterns
Patterns like "qwerty", "123456", "asdfgh" are in every hacker's dictionary. Even substitutions like p@ssw0rd are well known to attackers.
☁️
Secure Your Recovery Options
Your password reset email is as critical as your password itself. Use a strong, unique password for your primary email account above all else.
